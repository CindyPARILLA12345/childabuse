Resources and What to Do

Recognizing Signs of Abuse

Look for unexplained injuries, sudden behavior changes, fear of certain people or places, age-inappropriate sexual behavior, or poor hygiene.

How to Report Suspected Abuse

1. Document what you've observed with dates and details
2. Contact local child protective services or police
3. If the child is in immediate danger, call 911
4. Follow up to ensure action was taken

Supporting a Child Who Discloses Abuse

Remain calm, believe the child, reassure them it's not their fault, avoid questioning details, and report to authorities immediately.

Prevention Strategies

Teach children about body safety, establish open communication, monitor online activity, know the warning signs, and educate yourself and others.
